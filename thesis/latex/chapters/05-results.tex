\chapter{Results}\label{ch:results}

This chapter reports the pre-registered confirmatory study. The four
confirmatory hypotheses (H1, H2, H3, H5) are corrected together by Holm across
the campaigns; H4 is a descriptive frontier (not a falsifiable member); H6 was
not attempted (\S\ref{sec:h6}). Each section states its \emph{registered bar
first}, then the outcome against that bar --- a hypothesis counts as supported
only if it is both Holm-significant \emph{and} clears its registered
effect-size / reliability bar. The confirmatory family is corrected and
adjudicated together in \S\ref{sec:family-holm}.

\section{H1 --- Dose-response of the east-west tail}\label{sec:h1}

\textbf{Registered bar.} H1 predicts that median east-west (inter-service) p95
latency increases \emph{monotonically} in the achieved cross-node fraction $f$
across the designed levels $\{0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0\}$. Primary test: Page's
$L$ trend test (tie-corrected), session-condition medians of the pre-chaos
east-west p95 as the unit. The smallest effect size of interest (SESOI),
frozen pre-registration, is a \textbf{$\geq 15\,\%$ increase} from $f=0$ to
$f=1$; a statistically detectable but sub-SESOI trend is reported as below the
bar, not as support.

\textbf{Campaign.} Eight complete-block sessions (online-boutique, $r=1$,
\texttt{pod-delete} churn), each visiting all five $f$-levels in randomized
order; $5\times 3 = 15$ iterations per session, all \texttt{doctor --strict}
clean, all $f$-targets hit exactly.

\textbf{Result --- trend detected, but below the SESOI.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lr}
\toprule
Page's $L$ & $L = 410$, $z = 3.54$ \\
$p$ (one-sided, uncorrected) & $0.0002$ \\
per-level median p95 (ms) & 35.74 / 38.60 / 41.40 / 39.55 / 40.51 \\
$f{=}0 \rightarrow f{=}1$ effect & \textbf{$+13.35\,\%$} \\
$15\,\%$ SESOI & \textbf{not met} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

A monotone-increasing trend is detected ($p = 0.0002$; Holm-adjusted in
\S\ref{sec:family-holm}), but the total effect ($+13.35\,\%$) sits below the
registered $15\,\%$ SESOI. The medians rise then plateau/dip slightly at
$f{=}0.75$ (39.55\,ms) --- neither a strict monotone climb nor the clean
two-regime step that would have triggered the distinct registered
``threshold, not dose-response'' outcome. \textbf{H1 is therefore not supported:}
the dose-response is real and statistically significant but practically
negligible at the registered bar (Figure~\ref{fig:h1}).

\begin{figure}[tb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.78\textwidth]{fig-h1-dose-response.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H1 --- dose-response of the east-west tail} (C1, $r{=}1$,
    \texttt{pod-delete}). Per-level grand-median east-west p95 rises then
    plateaus/dips at $f{=}0.75$; the $f{=}0\rightarrow1$ change is $+13.35\,\%$,
    below the registered $15\,\%$ SESOI (dashed), so H1 is not supported despite
    a Holm-significant Page's $L$ trend.}
  \label{fig:h1}
\end{figure}

\section{H2 --- Placement-dependence and the DNS intervention}\label{sec:h2}

\textbf{Registered bar.} H2 is a two-part, both-must-pass conjunction over the
during-churn UDP-conntrack drop (\texttt{udp\_conntrack\_drop\_entries}):
\textbf{(a) placement-dependence} --- spread ($f{=}1$) shows a \emph{larger}
drop than packed ($f{=}0$), paired; and \textbf{(b) DNS mechanism} --- enabling
the NodeLocal DNSCache shrinks the spread placement's drop by $\geq 50\,\%$.
Both arms must pass for H2 to hold.

\textbf{Campaign.} Fourteen sessions (7 cache-off, 7 cache-on), $r=1$, placements
$f{=}0$ (packed) / $f{=}1$ (spread), \texttt{pod-delete} churn; all 14 accepted,
untainted, strict-clean.

\textbf{Result --- the DNS arm passes; the placement arm reverses.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.30\textwidth}p{0.34\textwidth}r}
\toprule
arm & result & one-sided $p$ \\
\midrule
(a) placement-dependence (cache-off) & \textbf{packed $>$ spread in 7/7 pairs} (packed median 11153.4, spread 8929.1) --- direction \textbf{reversed} & 0.98875 \\
(b) DNS mechanism (within-spread) & \textbf{78.0\,\% median} shrinkage (all 7 pairs 61--85\,\%) --- \textbf{passes} & 0.01125 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Conjunction = False; H2 is not supported.} Arm (b) is strongly
confirmed --- the DNS-cache intervention removes the conntrack drop. Arm (a)
fails on \emph{direction}, and the reversal is robust and monotone: packed
exceeds spread in every one of the 7 cache-off pairs (two-sided Wilcoxon at the
$n=7$ floor, $p = 0.0225$). Co-locating a service's replicas on one node
\emph{concentrates} the per-node conntrack churn when \texttt{pod-delete} hits
that node, producing a \emph{larger} during-churn UDP drop than spread
placement, which distributes the same replicas across nodes. Placement
\emph{does} matter for the conntrack drop --- significantly, but in the opposite
direction to the registered prediction. A registered secondary confirms the
mechanism's generality: the packed placement's cache-off drop (median 11153.4)
also collapses to $\approx 0$ with the cache on (median $-105.2$;
$p = 0.01125$) --- the intervention removes the drop under \emph{both}
placements (Figure~\ref{fig:h2}).

\begin{figure}[tb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{fig-h2-conntrack-dns.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H2 --- conntrack placement-dependence and the DNS
    intervention} (C3). (a) The cache-off during-churn UDP-conntrack drop is
    \emph{larger} under packing than spreading --- reversed against the
    registered prediction, packed $>$ spread in all 7 pairs. (b) Enabling the
    NodeLocal DNSCache collapses the packed drop to $\approx 0$ and shrinks the
    spread drop by a $78\,\%$ median, removing it under both placements: arm (b)
    passes, but the reversed direction (a) fails the conjunction.}
  \label{fig:h2}
\end{figure}

\section{H3 --- Replication rescue under node-drain}\label{sec:h3}

\textbf{Registered bar.} H3 predicts an \emph{interaction}: replication
($r{=}3$) rescues availability under node-drain only when replicas do not share
the failure domain (anti-affine $r{=}3 \ll r{=}1$; packed $r{=}3 \approx r{=}1$).
Two co-primary outcomes --- trough depth and user-route error rate --- combined
both-must-pass; each requires (i) a significant ART-ANOVA $r\times\text{mode}$
interaction, (ii) the anti-affine rescue margin met, and (iii) the
packed$\approx r{=}1$ equivalence (TOST) control.

\textbf{Campaign.} 24 node-drain sessions ($3$ cells $\times 8$): r1-packed,
r3-packed, r3-anti-affine, on the capacity-feasible round-robin packed
instrument; all 24 accepted and untainted.

\textbf{Result --- the error face rescues; the depth face cannot adjudicate.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{p{0.20\textwidth}rrrp{0.26\textwidth}}
\toprule
co-primary & r1 & r3-pk & r3-anti & verdict vs bar \\
\midrule
trough depth (frac) & 0.0909 & 0.0909 & 0.0455 & interaction sig ($p{=}0.0065$); rescue $0.0455 < 0.0909$ margin $\rightarrow$ \textbf{not met} \\
user-route error & 0.6316 & 0.6316 & 0.0000 & interaction $p \approx 0$; rescue $0.6316 \geq 0.302$ $\rightarrow$ \textbf{met}; TOST within band \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Conjunction = False; H3 is not supported} --- but the single failing
criterion is one the depth co-primary \emph{could not adjudicate by
construction}. Both packing controls pass (packed $r{=}3 \approx r{=}1$ exactly
on both faces), so the instrument behaves as designed. On the
\textbf{user-route error face}, anti-affine $r{=}3$ fully rescues the error rate
($0.632 \rightarrow 0.0$; interaction $p \approx 0$; rescue $\gg$ the 0.302
margin) --- strong, significant directional support. On the \textbf{trough-depth
face}, the interaction is significant ($p = 0.0065$) but the anti-affine rescue
(0.0455) falls below the 0.0909 margin; under round-robin spread, draining one
node costs $r{=}1$ only $\approx 1$ pod, so the $r{=}1$ depth (0.0909) equals the
registered one-pod margin --- the metric's dynamic range coincides with the bar
it must beat, leaving no room for a rescue to register. The depth co-primary is
reported exactly as registered (not retuned); the substantive result is the
error face, where replication rescues user-visible availability when replicas
are spread (Figure~\ref{fig:h3}).

\begin{figure}[tb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{fig-h3-replication-rescue.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H3 --- replication rescue under node-drain} (C2), across the
    three cells. (a) Trough depth: the anti-affine rescue (0.0455) falls below
    the registered one-pod margin (0.0909, dashed), which the $r{=}1$ and
    $r{=}3$-packed cells sit on by construction --- the depth face cannot
    adjudicate. (b) User-route error: anti-affine $r{=}3$ fully rescues
    ($0.632\rightarrow0$), clearing the 0.302 margin. The conjunction fails only
    on the depth co-primary it could not test.}

\end{figure}

\section{H4 --- The placement frontier (descriptive)}\label{sec:h4}

\textbf{Protocol (registered, descriptive --- no $p$-value).} For each designed
placement, the \emph{latency face} (pre-chaos east-west p95 tail) is plotted
against the \emph{availability face} (during-chaos trough depth in pods +
user-route error rate), and the non-dominated set is reported under frozen
margins ($\delta_{\text{latency}} = 4.4$\,ms, $\delta_{\text{depth}} = 1.0$
pod, $\delta_{\text{error}} = 0.302$; dominance requires beating on the latency
face \emph{and} both availability DVs).

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrrrc}
\toprule
placement (r=1, pod-delete) & EW p95 [ms] & depth [pods] & user err & non-dom.? \\
\midrule
$f{=}0$ (packed) & 35.74 & 1.0 & 0.04 & yes \\
$f{=}0.25$ & 38.60 & 1.0 & 0.04 & yes \\
$f{=}0.5$ & 41.40 & 1.0 & 0.04 & yes \\
$f{=}0.75$ & 39.55 & 1.0 & 0.04 & yes \\
$f{=}1$ (spread) & 40.51 & 1.0 & 0.01 & yes \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Non-dominated set: all 5/5 placements.} The frontier is degenerate on
the availability face: \texttt{pod-delete} removes a single pod by
construction, so trough depth is $\approx 1$ pod for every placement and the
error rate is uniformly low --- neither availability DV separates placements by
its margin. The latency face \emph{does} vary (packed 35.7\,ms vs mid 41.4\,ms,
$> \delta_{\text{latency}}$, non-overlapping CIs --- the H1 signal), but a
single-face advantage cannot establish margin-dominance under the all-DV rule,
so the protocol correctly crowns no winner. The two-face
latency$\leftrightarrow$availability trade-off the frontier was designed to
reveal is \textbf{not realizable from the collected data}: the fault that varies
the availability face (node-drain) has no latency face, and the fault with a
latency face (pod-delete) produces no availability-face variation
(Figure~\ref{fig:h4}).

\begin{figure}[tb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{fig-h4-frontier.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H4 --- descriptive placement frontier} (C1,
    \texttt{pod-delete}). All five placements are non-dominated (red rings): the
    latency face varies (east-west p95 $35.7$--$41.4$\,ms) but the availability
    face is degenerate --- trough depth is $\approx1$ pod and the error rate
    uniformly low --- so no placement dominates on all faces.}
  \label{fig:h4}
\end{figure}

\section{H5 --- Layered scorecard reliability}\label{sec:h5}

\textbf{Registered bar.} H5 evaluates a layered resilience scorecard against a
\textbf{naive single-aggregate baseline} via condition-level test-retest
reliability (ICC), requiring a conjunction of the \textbf{availability} and
\textbf{mechanism} sub-scores each clearing an absolute bar of $\text{ICC}\geq
0.5$.

\textbf{Result --- mechanism reliable, availability not.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lrlc}
\toprule
sub-score & ICC & 95\,\% CI & $\geq 0.5$? \\
\midrule
availability (required) & \textbf{0.180} & [0.042, 0.468] & \textbf{no} \\
mechanism (required) & \textbf{0.994} & [0.635, 0.999] & yes \\
naive aggregate baseline & 0.066 & [0.027, 0.343] & --- \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Required conjunction: FAIL; H5 is not supported.} This is a sensible
registered falsification, not a method failure: \texttt{pod-delete} churn
produces no sustained endpoint outage, so the availability layer has little
reproducible between-condition signal to estimate (that face is fault-matched
to node-drain). The mechanism layer is the headline reliability result --- ICC
0.994 versus the naive aggregate's 0.066: the layered scorecard's
conntrack-reconvergence sub-score is far more test-retest reliable than a single
aggregate score. The verdict is robust to the analysis's taint-handling choice
(the conjunction fails either way). A single-campaign 0.994 with a wide-ish
lower CI bound is strong but is re-evaluated across environments before being
over-read (Figure~\ref{fig:h5}).

\begin{figure}[tb]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{fig-h5-scorecard-icc.png}
  \caption{\textbf{H5 --- layered-scorecard test-retest reliability} (C1). The
    mechanism sub-score (ICC 0.994) clears the registered $\text{ICC}\geq0.5$ bar
    (dashed); the required availability sub-score does not (0.180), so H5 is not
    supported. Both far exceed the naive single-aggregate baseline (0.066). Bars
    show 95\,\% bootstrap CIs.}
  \label{fig:h5}
\end{figure}

\section{Confirmatory family --- Holm capstone}\label{sec:family-holm}

The four confirmatory hypotheses are corrected together by Holm at
$\alpha = 0.05$, each member's family-input $p$ read verbatim from its own
analysis driver.

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{llrrc}
\toprule
hyp & primary test & input $p$ & Holm-adj $p$ & supported? \\
\midrule
H1 & dose-response (Page's $L$) & 0.0002 & \textbf{0.0008} & no (effect $<$ SESOI) \\
H2 & placement + DNS & 0.98875 & 0.98875 & no (placement reversed) \\
H3 & replication rescue & 0.0065 & \textbf{0.0195} & no (rescue margin unmet) \\
H5 & layered scorecard ICC & 0.2501 & 0.5002 & no (availability ICC $<0.5$) \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\textbf{Family verdict: no confirmatory hypothesis is supported.} Two primaries
(H1's trend, H3's interaction) survive Holm as statistically significant, but
Holm-significance is necessary, not sufficient: each member also carries a
registered effect-size / reliability bar the $p$-value cannot speak to, and
every member fails its bar or its significance. The consistent, positive thread
across all three campaigns is the \emph{mechanism}: the UDP-conntrack
reconvergence signature is real, individually significant in every campaign
(H2's placement effect, H3's interaction, H5's mechanism ICC 0.994), and
movable by placement and by the DNS intervention. What does \emph{not} hold, at
the pre-registered bar, is that this mechanism translates into the
user-visible placement, availability, and dose-response advantages the
hypotheses predicted. The discussion (\S\ref{sec:decoupling}) develops this as
the study's central finding: a real mechanism, rigorously bounded in reach.

\section{External validity --- a second workload}\label{sec:external-validity}

\textbf{Motivation.} The single largest threat to these results
(\S\ref{sec:generalizes}) is that they come from one workload. As an exploratory
check --- \emph{outside} the frozen confirmatory family, so no multiplicity
correction is owed --- the two placement-bearing studies (H1 dose-response and
H3 replication rescue) were replicated on a second, deliberately different
application: DeathStarBench hotelReservation, a deep frontend fan-out with
per-service datastore pairs and Go gRPC over Consul service discovery, against
online-boutique's flatter topology and ClusterIP routing.

\textbf{Campaign.} The protocols were identical: 8 H1 complete-block sessions
(\texttt{pod-delete} on the search path, $r{=}1$, five $f$-levels $\times$ 3
iterations) and 24 H3 sessions (\texttt{node-drain}; cells r1-packed,
r3-packed, r3-anti-affine $\times$ 8). All 32 sessions were
\texttt{doctor --strict} clean with pristine provenance; 1 of 144 iterations was
excluded on an \texttt{app\_ready\_timeout} taint.\footnote{Measuring
hotelReservation required general ChaosProbe fixes, not changes to the
experimental design or the system under test: a wget-capable readiness-probe
pod, a static-topology fallback for the cross-node fraction on Consul/gRPC
services that expose no service-address environment dependencies, and sustained
warm-up load through the readiness gate. A prior scoping attempt mis-attributed
the resulting gate timeouts to the workload's gRPC keepalive recovery; the cause
was these tooling gaps, and once closed the campaign ran clean.}

\textbf{Result --- both arms corroborate online-boutique.}

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
study & registered bar & hotelReservation outcome \\
\midrule
H1 dose-response & monotone east-west p95 increase, $\geq 15\,\%$ SESOI &
  Page's $L$ $p = 0.99$ --- \textbf{no increase} \\
H3 replication rescue & $r{\times}$mode interaction + rescue $\geq$ margins &
  \texttt{CONJUNCTION = False} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

H1 on hotelReservation shows \emph{no} dose-response of the east-west tail: the
registered one-sided trend test is non-significant ($p = 0.99$) and the per-level
medians ($9.34 / 6.44 / 6.32 / 7.10 / 5.40$\,ms) sit in a tight band with, if
anything, a mild \emph{decrease}. This is the same negative reading as
online-boutique's sub-SESOI H1 (\S\ref{sec:h1}), slightly strengthened --- no
increase at all on the deeper topology. H3 reaches online-boutique's top-line
verdict (\texttt{CONJUNCTION = False}) by the same route --- a \emph{significant}
$r{\times}$mode interaction on both co-primaries ($p \approx 0$) with anti-affine
$r{=}3$ the directionally best arm on every measure (trough recovery $15$\,s
versus $r{=}1$'s $45$\,s, during-chaos user-error $0.0$ versus $0.21$) and the
packing controls holding --- though the conjunction fails more broadly here:
where online-boutique cleared the user-error margin and failed only on trough
depth (\S\ref{sec:h3}), hotelReservation clears \emph{neither} margin (depth
$0.044 < 0.053$, error $0.212 < 0.302$).

\textbf{Reading.} The cross-workload picture mirrors the within-workload one
(\S\ref{sec:family-holm}): the \emph{mechanism} --- spreading replicas across
failure domains shrinks the node-drain blast radius and speeds recovery --- is
robust and visible on both applications, while the \emph{strong placement and
margin-clearing claims} hold on neither. That a second, structurally different
workload reproduces both the below-SESOI dose-response and the
significant-but-margin-short rescue is the strongest available evidence that the
study's central finding --- a real mechanism, rigorously bounded in reach --- is
not an artifact of one application's topology. This arm is exploratory and its
verdicts are not folded into the Holm family; its full numbers, per-session
provenance, and frozen integrity manifest are recorded in the committed C1/C2
hotelReservation campaign reports alongside the deposited raw dataset.

\section{H6 --- direction transfer (not attempted)}\label{sec:h6}

H6 (an exploratory, non-family secondary on iptables-mode direction transfer)
was \textbf{not attempted}: the cluster ran the IPVS proxier rather than the
iptables mode the hypothesis presumes, so the comparison was de-scoped before
collection (recorded in the deviations log). It supports no claim and is
reported here only for completeness of the registered set.
